ABOUT

The Tenderloin Stop the Violence Movement is led by a coalition of young people in our community. After losing our closest friend in April 2017 to unnecessary violence, we gathered in our grief and decided to make immediate change. Our interest in change led to the production of the “T.L. Stop the Violence Event” to build relationships with law enforcement, and create mentorship, employment, housing and mental health services. Many young people in the T.L. connect through hip hop, bringing the different nationalities together through music. We believe saving lives through multicultural hip hop connections is our future.



History

The history of the Tenderloin has been rich with music, theater, arts and culture since the 1940s. In July 2008,the area was designated as a historic district on the National Register of Historic Places. Over time, social issues and crime has also been present. According to a January 6th, 2014 Chronicle article, the Tenderloin is seeing its highest rates of crime and violence. Over the years there has been an uprising in violence. In 2017 up to present day the violence is out of control. The Stop the Violence event helped us to begin the process of getting back to our roots through music, arts and culture.



Displacement

The Tenderloin is considered an undesirable place to live, which has driven down the costs of rent. Immigrants and poor communities from all over San Francisco transcended into the Tenderloin for low cost living in SRO’s, subsidized housing and weekly hotel rentals. Homelessness due to the rapid gentrification in San Francisco has hit the Fillmore, Bayview/Hunter’s Point, Mission, Visitation Valley and Lake View the hardest. These neighborhoods were known as low income communities and suffer from the plague of violence, drugs and mental health issues. Generations of residents from San Francisco are being displaced and impacts their children, economic welfare, social status and health.



PROGRAMS

As the violence continues to erupt in the T.L. young people, the community, law enforcement and city officials have decided to gather to produce the “T.L. Stop the Violence Event.” The serious efforts of planning this event will not only save lives but will empower, educate and connect the T.L. for a better future. Moving forward the event will produce mentorship opportunities, housing, employment and mental health resources. Please see examples below:



Mentorship

  • Linking the youth to professionals in various fields to introduce them to other working industries. (Connections Through Specific Agencies)
  • Linking youth to internships in local law enforcement. (District Attorney, Public Defender, SFPD, Sheriff’s Department, Adult Probation and Reentry Council)
  • Building mentorship between elders and youth.
  • Develop relationships with local prisons and jails. (Connections Through Specific Agencies)
  • Prison programming through visits, follow up and education. (Connections Through Specific Agencies)
  • Reentry Mentoring. Informing people who are leaving the system how to comfortably reenter society. (Connections Through Specific Agencies)


Employment

  • Production of this event will create immediate employment and internship opportunities (Event production, social media, promotion, journalism, etc.)
  • Funding approved for 5 internships for 14 – 17 years old. $600 will be provided to each youth on the completion of a one month event production internship. Provided by Renaissance Parents of Success.
  • Reading, writing and Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEAM). (Connecting to free college at City College or other educational institutions and resources)
  • Mini job fair with companies that will hire at the event. (Connections Through Specific Agencies)
  • Post event continue to work with employment agencies to hire locals in the area.
  • Current job opportunities in law enforcement.
  • Consistently advertising local job positions in the T.L. neighborhood.
  • Introducing different industries such as security, culinary and construction.
  • Clothing program (Connecting with clothing business and programs that can supply professional attire.)
  • Immigration services. (Connections Through Specific Agencies)


Housing

  • Working with housing organizations to locate housing.
  • Working with foster care service providers to provide housing.
  • Shelters for women and children.
  • Identify housing for 18 - 30.
  • Emergency housing, shelters, churches, hotel vouchers, transitional housing
  • Immigration services(Connection Through Agencies)


Mental Health

  • Identify mental health needs and services
  • Working with the City and S.F. Department of Public Health.
  • Connecting youth to resources in area and throughout.
  • Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) resources.
  • Holistic health care (Connections Through Specific Agencies)
  • Community garden (Identify area in T.L. to create or work on existing garden)
  • Art healing (Music, painting, photography, writing, etc.)
  • Immigration services (Connections Through Specific Agencies)
